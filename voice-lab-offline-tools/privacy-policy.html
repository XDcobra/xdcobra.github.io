2. Overview

Voice Lab: STT, TTS & More (listed on the App Store and Google Play; in-app branding: VoiceLab) is an on-device speech toolkit for exploring features such as speech-to-text, text-to-speech, translation, voice cloning, voice activity detection, diarization, enhancement, and separation. You can use the app without registration and without creating a login account. This privacy policy explains which data is processed when you use the app and for which purposes.

The app does not provide login accounts or user profiles. We do not ask for your name or email to use the app. The app creates a persistent random device identifier stored in the iOS Keychain (or the Android equivalent secure storage). This identifier is not your name or email. It is used to link subscription restore (RevenueCat), anonymous usage metering, crash diagnostics, and optional feedback on the same device over time.

Third-party advertising SDKs may additionally process identifiers such as the Advertising ID (IDFA on iOS) when you consent, as described in section 3.2.

3. Data collection and processing

3.1 Data processed locally by the app

The app processes the following data locally on your device:

  • Audio files or recordings you provide: Audio is processed on-device for speech tasks such as STT, TTS, VAD, diarization, enhancement, and separation.
  • Model files and metadata: Downloaded model files, checksums, and local manifest data (e.g. download timestamp, last-used timestamp) are stored locally to manage offline use.
  • Model settings and app preferences: Selected models, decoding parameters, and UI preferences are stored locally.

These locally stored data are not accessed by us as the provider and are not transmitted to our own servers. We only process personal data if you contact us directly, for example via email.

We do not train machine learning or speech models on your audio, transcripts, synthesized speech, or other session content. On-device inference uses pre-trained models you download; your recordings and generated outputs are not sent to us or to third-party model hosts for training or model-improvement purposes.

Data that may be processed by Crashlytics includes:

Legal basis: We rely on our legitimate interest in ensuring the technical stability and quality of the app (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR). You may object to this processing by contacting us; we will then review whether we can restrict or discontinue sending crash and error data in your case.

3.4 Model downloads from GitHub Releases

The app lets you download speech models directly from the official sherpa-onnx GitHub Releases page. These downloads are initiated by you and stored locally on your device so the models can be used offline.

Data that may be processed when downloading models:

  • IP address
  • Device and network information (e.g. device type, operating system, connection type)
  • Download metadata (e.g. file requested, timestamp, size)

To verify integrity, the app may compute cryptographic checksums (SHA-256) of downloaded model files on your device. These checksum calculations are performed locally and are not transmitted to our servers.

Downloads are served by GitHub (GitHub, Inc., 88 Colin P Kelly Jr St, San Francisco, CA 94107, USA) or its infrastructure providers. GitHub may process the data above in accordance with its privacy policy.

GitHub privacy information:
https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/privacy-policies/github-privacy-statement

3.5 Usage metering and configuration (Firebase Firestore)

To enforce free-tier usage limits and keep limits consistent across reinstalls on the same device, the app syncs anonymous usage counters with Google Firebase Firestore. These counters reflect how much of the free monthly budget you have used (for example speech minutes and text characters). They do not include audio recordings, transcripts, synthesized speech, or other content of your sessions.

Data processed may include:

  • The persistent device identifier (see section 2) as the document key
  • Numeric counters per calendar month (speech minutes used, text characters used, including free-tier and total tallies)
  • Timestamps of the last sync
  • Technical write identifiers used to avoid duplicate counting when offline

The app may also read remote configuration from Firestore (for example app_config/limits) to adjust free-tier limits without requiring an app update. This read does not transmit your audio or text content.

Purposes: Operating the free tier fairly, preventing abuse, and applying published usage limits.

Legal basis: Legitimate interest in operating the free tier and performing the contract for free-tier use (Art. 6(1)(b) and Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR), where applicable.

To request erasure of Firestore documents linked to your device identifier, contact us using the email address in section 12.

3.6 In-app feedback (Firebase Firestore)

If you choose to send feedback (for example from Settings or via thumbs up/down on a result screen), the app transmits only what you type together with technical metadata. Feedback is private support input and is not published in the app or shown to other users.

Data processed may include:

  • Your comment text (subject to client-side profanity and spam checks)
  • The persistent device identifier (section 2)
  • Feature and model identifiers, app version, platform, and feedback type
  • Timestamps

No audio files or transcripts are attached to feedback submissions. Storage location: Firestore feedback collection.

Purposes: Improving the app, investigating issues, and understanding feature usage.

Legal basis: Your consent when you submit feedback (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR) and legitimate interest in product improvement (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR).

3.7 Hugging Face and custom model sources

You may initiate downloads of speech models from Hugging Face (huggingface.co) or from user-configured HTTPS URLs (custom model catalogs). These downloads are started by you; model files are stored locally on your device.

Data that may be processed when you download from these sources includes:

  • IP address and request metadata (handled by the remote host and its infrastructure)
  • Download URLs and file identifiers you request
  • An optional Hugging Face access token you enter in the app, stored locally on your device for authenticated downloads. This token is not an app login account or user profile.

No audio from your sessions is uploaded to Hugging Face or custom catalog hosts through these download flows. You are responsible for complying with each model’s license and the host’s terms of use.
